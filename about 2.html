About Toolhub

What Toolhub is

Toolhub is a growing collection of small, single-purpose web tools — calculators, converters, counters, and checkers — built for the moment you need a quick answer and don't want to install an app, create an account, or dig through a bloated page to get it. Each tool does one job, does it on the page you're already on, and gets out of your way. The goal is simple: open the tool, get the number or result you came for, move on.

How each tool is built and validated

Every tool starts from a real, well-defined formula or rule set — the kind you'd find in a textbook, an official guideline, or an established convention for that domain (finance, health, text, unit conversion, and so on). Before a tool ships, its logic is checked against known reference values and edge cases: boundary inputs, rounding behavior, and the scenarios most likely to trip up a naive implementation. When a tool's subject matter has real nuance — tax rules, medical estimates, financial projections — the page says so plainly, so the result is read as an estimate rather than professional advice.

Privacy by design

Every calculation runs locally in your browser using JavaScript. The numbers, dates, or text you type into a Toolhub tool are never sent to a server, logged, or stored anywhere outside your own device. There are no accounts, no forms to fill in, and no hidden network calls carrying your inputs elsewhere. The only things saved locally are small preferences — like your chosen theme or language — kept in your browser's own storage so the site remembers your settings on your next visit. Full details on data handling live on the Privacy Policy page.

Keeping Toolhub free

Toolhub has no subscription, no paywall, and no premium tier — every tool is free to use, with no limit on how many times you use it. Running the site and building new tools still costs time and money, so Toolhub is supported by advertising (Google AdSense) placed alongside the tools, and may display ads more broadly as the project grows. Ads are kept out of the way of the actual tool interface, and nothing about how a tool calculates its result depends on them. If you'd rather support the project directly, there's a link in the footer for that too.

Feedback and contact

Toolhub is a small, ongoing project, and it improves from real usage — a formula that's slightly off, a unit that's missing, a tool that would be genuinely useful to add. If you spot something wrong or have a suggestion, the fastest way to reach the maintainer is by email at jhighllight@gmail.com or through GitHub. Reports that include the exact tool, the input you used, and what you expected are the easiest to act on quickly.
