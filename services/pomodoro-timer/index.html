Frequently asked questions

What is the Pomodoro Technique, and why 25 minutes?

The Pomodoro Technique alternates 25 minutes of focus with 5-minute breaks — one set is a "pomodoro" — and inserts a longer break of about 15 minutes every 4 pomodoros. 25 minutes is short enough to start without resistance yet long enough for deep focus, so this pomodoro timer uses a 25-minute timer by default and rotates the cycle automatically. Press Start once and the focus-break switching is handled for you.

Can I change the focus and break lengths to my own rhythm (like 50/10)?

Yes. In the timer settings you can set focus time to 1–90 minutes, short breaks to 1–30, long breaks to 1–60, and the long-break interval to every 2–8 pomodoros. Build a 50-minute deep-work focus timer or a 90-minute study timer — out-of-range values are corrected automatically, and your settings are saved in the browser and restored on your next visit.

Is it accurate when the tab is in the background?

Yes. Instead of counting ticks, this focus timer stores the exact end time when a session starts and recomputes the remaining time from the clock on every update. Even if the browser throttles background tabs, the correct time shows the moment you return — and if the session already ended, it alerts and switches immediately. The remaining time also appears in the tab title so you can watch it from another window.

I can't hear the alert sound — why?

Browsers only allow sound after you interact with the page, so this timer arms the audio the moment you press Start. Focus endings use a higher beep (880Hz) and break endings a lower one (660Hz), so you can tell them apart without looking. If you still hear nothing, check the system volume, tab mute and your phone's silent switch. Without Web Audio support, a notice appears and the alert stays visual.

Where is my daily pomodoro count stored?

Today's count and your timer settings are stored only in your browser (localStorage) — nothing is sent to a server. No sign-up or install is needed, and the count automatically resets to zero when the date changes. Clearing browser data or opening the page on another device starts a fresh count.
