Frequently asked questions

Does a high BMI always mean I'm obese?

Body mass index (BMI) is calculated from height and weight alone, so it cannot see muscle mass. A muscular athlete can have a high BMI while carrying very little body fat. Use the number from this obesity calculator as a screening reference, and consult a health professional for an accurate body-composition assessment.

Why do the WHO and Asia-Pacific cut-offs differ?

The WHO cut-offs (overweight at 25) were built mainly on Western populations and can underestimate real risk for Asian people, who tend to carry more body fat at the same BMI and face higher metabolic-disease risk. That is why bodies such as the Korean Society for the Study of Obesity (2022) apply stricter cut-offs — pre-obese from 23, obesity class I from 25. This BMI calculator always shows both, estimates your region to pick one as the default interpretation, and lets you switch with a link under the result.

Why is the healthy BMI range 18.5–22.9?

The Asia-Pacific standard defines 18.5–22.9 as the normal range based on epidemiological studies in Asian populations, where chronic-disease risk (cardiovascular disease, diabetes) is lowest in that band. Under the WHO standard the normal range is a little wider, 18.5–24.9. The healthy weight range shown is derived from the standard you are currently viewing and the height you entered.

Can I calculate in pounds (lb) and feet (ft)?

Yes. This calculator reads your browser language and region to pick a unit system automatically — ft·in·lb for imperial regions such as the US and UK, cm·kg elsewhere. You can switch anytime with the toggle at the top, and any value you already entered is converted for you, so there's no need to re-type. Your choice is remembered in the browser for next time. BMI itself is a dimensionless index, so you get the same value in either unit.

Is my height and weight sent to a server?

No. This body mass index calculator runs every calculation inside your browser. Your inputs are never sent to an external server; they are stored only in the browser's localStorage for convenience on your next visit (height and weight, unit system, interpretation standard). For the same reason the values are not put in the URL — so opening a link never leaves your numbers in a server access log. You can clear the stored values anytime from your browser settings.

How is a BMI calculator different from a body-fat calculator?

A BMI (body mass index) calculator estimates weight status quickly from height and weight only. A body-fat calculator measures the actual percentage of body fat and needs separate equipment such as a DEXA scan or bioelectrical impedance (BIA). BMI is a useful fast screen; body-fat percentage is for more precise body-composition analysis.
