About this fuel cost calculator

This fuel cost calculator estimates what a drive will cost in fuel from three things you already know: the distance, your car's fuel economy, and the price at the pump. It's handy for planning a road trip, budgeting a daily commute, or working out a fair share when several people ride together — and it mixes metric and imperial units freely, so your dashboard numbers go straight in.

How to use it

Enter the trip distance and pick km or miles. Add your fuel economy in whatever unit your car shows — km/L, L/100km, or MPG (US or UK). Choose a currency and type the fuel price per liter or per gallon. Tick Round trip to count the drive there and back, and set the passenger count to split the cost. The total, fuel needed, cost per distance, and per-passenger share appear at once.

How the cost is worked out

Under the hood every unit is converted to liters and kilometers, then fuel needed = distance ÷ fuel economy and cost = fuel × price. Drive 320 km in a car that does 12 km/L with fuel at 1.75 per liter: you burn 320 ÷ 12 = 26.7 liters, so the trip costs 26.7 × 1.75 = about 46.70, or roughly 0.15 per km. Tick round trip and the distance doubles to 640 km, pushing fuel to 53.3 liters and the cost to about 93.30.

Tips & good to know

Real-world fuel economy drifts from the sticker figure: city traffic, hard acceleration, a loaded car, air conditioning, and cold weather all burn more, while steady highway cruising burns less. For a trip that mixes conditions, use your car's recent average rather than its best-case number. Treat the result as a solid estimate for budgeting and cost-sharing, not a to-the-cent guarantee — and re-check the pump price, since it changes day to day.

How is fuel cost calculated?

This fuel cost calculator works out total cost = distance ÷ fuel economy × fuel price. Everything is normalized to liters and kilometers first, so you can mix units freely: distance in km or miles, economy in km/L, L/100km or MPG, and price per liter or per gallon. Turn on round trip to double the distance, and split the total across passengers.

MPG vs L/100km vs km/L — which unit should I use?

Use whatever your car shows. The US and UK measure in miles per gallon (MPG), most of Europe uses L/100km (liters per 100 km), and Korea and Japan often use km/L (kilometers per liter). This gas cost calculator converts all of them, so pick the one on your dashboard and enter the number as-is.

What's the difference between a US gallon and a UK gallon?

A US gallon is about 3.785 liters and a UK (imperial) gallon is about 4.546 liters — roughly 20% larger. That gap affects both price per gallon and MPG. Choose MPG (US) or MPG (UK), and per US gal or per UK gal, to match where you fill up so the trip fuel calculator stays accurate.

How do I split fuel cost or add a round trip?

Tick round trip to count the drive there and back (distance × 2). Set the number of passengers with the − and + buttons, and the calculator shows the cost per passenger so everyone can chip in a fair share.

Are live fuel prices included?

No. You enter the current fuel price yourself, so the result is always up to date and never depends on an external server. Every calculation runs locally in your browser — nothing you type is uploaded, saved, or shared.
