How is fuel cost calculated?

This fuel cost calculator works out total cost = distance ÷ fuel economy × fuel price. Pick your country first and it sets the units and currency the way you actually buy fuel — miles, MPG and price per gallon in the US; kilometers, L/100km and price per liter across most of Europe; km/L in Korea, Japan, Brazil and India. Everything is normalized to liters and kilometers internally, so you only type three numbers: distance, fuel economy and price.

MPG vs L/100km vs km/L — which unit should I use?

Whichever your car shows — and your country already picked it for you. The US measures in miles per gallon (MPG), the UK uses imperial MPG, most of Europe and China use L/100km (liters per 100 km), and Korea, Japan, Brazil and India use km/L (kilometers per liter). This gas cost calculator converts all of them, so read the number off your dashboard and enter it as-is. Driving an imported car? Change the unit next to the field and the rest keeps working.

Why does the UK price fuel in liters but rate cars in MPG?

Britain sells fuel by the liter but still rates cars in miles per imperial gallon — so a UK trip needs both units at once, which is exactly what this calculator does when you pick the United Kingdom. Watch out for the two gallons: a US gallon is about 3.785 liters and a UK (imperial) gallon about 4.546 liters, roughly 20% larger. So 40 MPG (UK) is only about 33 MPG (US) — never compare the two figures directly.

How do I split fuel cost or add a round trip?

Open "Round trip and passengers" under the price. Tick round trip to count the drive there and back (distance × 2), and set the number of passengers with the − and + buttons — the calculator then shows the cost per passenger so everyone can chip in a fair share. Both are optional: leave them alone for a plain one-way estimate.

Are live fuel prices included?

No. You enter the current fuel price yourself, so the result is always up to date and never depends on an external server. Every calculation runs locally in your browser — nothing you type is uploaded, saved, or shared.
